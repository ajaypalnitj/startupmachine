About Color Palettes

            
A color palette is a collection of colors used in design projects to create visual harmony and consistency. The right color palette can evoke specific emotions, enhance brand recognition, and improve user experience.

            
            
Palette Types

            
    
                
  • Analogous: Colors that are adjacent to each other on the color wheel, creating a harmonious and cohesive look.
    • 
                
  • Monochromatic: Different shades, tones, and tints of a single color, offering a clean and elegant appearance.
    • 
                
  • Triadic: Three colors equally spaced around the color wheel, providing strong visual contrast while maintaining harmony.
    • 
                
  • Complementary: Colors opposite each other on the color wheel, creating maximum contrast and visual impact.
    • 
                
  • Split Complementary: A base color plus two colors adjacent to its complement, offering high contrast with less tension than complementary schemes.
    •
